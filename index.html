Mission of the sikh faith

The mission of the Sikh faith is to uplift humanity through devotion, equality, and selfless service. Guided by the teachings of Guru Nanak Dev Ji and the Ten Sikh Gurus, the Sikh path promotes living a truthful, disciplined, and compassionate life.

Core Principles

The Three Pillars of Sikhism Guru Nanak Dev Ji formalized three basic guidlines for Sikhs:

Naam Japna

Remembering and meditating on God

Sikhs strive to keep God’s Name (Waheguru) in their heart and actions at all times, living with humility,
gratitude, and spiritual awareness.

Kirat Karni

Earning an honest living

Sikhs are committed to working with integrity, without deceit, exploitation, or dishonesty.

Vand Chakna

Sharing with Others

Sikhs are called to share their earnings, time, and resources with the needy and to support community
well-being.

Universal Values

These principles emphasize the unity of humankind, selfless service to others, upholding justice, fostering a strong sense of community, and living an honest life based on truthful conduct.

Equality of All
Seva (Selfless Service)

Purpose of the Sikh Mission

To nurture a world where:
• God’s presence is remembered in every heart
• All humans are treated with fairness and respect
• The hungry are fed and the suffering are comforted
• Life is lived with courage, humility, and righteousness
• Peace, harmony, and unity prevail among all people
